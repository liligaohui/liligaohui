Automation & AI

Python · Risk · SFU SIAS

Risk & Return Monitoring Automation

A Python automation suite that replaced manual Excel-based risk and performance reporting for the SFU SIAS global equity portfolio — cutting reporting time from days to minutes.

+

The Problem

The SFU SIAS portfolio team was manually compiling weekly risk reports in Excel — pulling data from Bloomberg, calculating active risk, tracking benchmark deviations, and building attribution tables by hand. This process consumed days of analyst time each cycle and introduced inconsistencies from manual data entry.

With a CAD 3M+ portfolio, the delay between data and insights was a real operational risk.

How I Solved It

Built a Python pipeline using pandas, numpy, and matplotlib that ingests raw Bloomberg exports and outputs structured reports automatically.

  • Automated active risk calculation and flagging against IPS thresholds
  • Built return attribution separating sector vs. stock selection effects
  • Integrated benchmark comparison with automatic deviation alerts
  • Output templates feed directly into the weekly investment committee deck

Impact

◆Reporting time reduced from 2–3 days to under 30 minutes per cycle
◆Eliminated manual transcription errors across 30+ equity positions
◆Enabled real-time risk threshold monitoring for the first time
◆Adopted by the full SIAS team as the standard reporting workflow

DCF · Valuation · Equity Research

DCF Valuation Models

Built bottom-up DCF models for equity investment decisions within the SIAS portfolio — covering revenue drivers, capital structure assumptions, and multi-scenario sensitivity analysis.

+

The Problem

Sector analysts needed DCF models that were technically correct, robust enough to withstand scrutiny in investment committee presentations, and flexible enough to stress-test key assumptions quickly. Generic templates were insufficient — each company required a model tailored to its business model, growth drivers, and capital structure.

How I Solved It

  • Revenue build from volume and pricing drivers, not simple growth rate assumptions
  • WACC built from first principles — cost of equity via CAPM, cost of debt from credit spreads
  • Terminal value via both Gordon Growth and EV/EBITDA exit multiple for cross-validation
  • Full sensitivity tables across WACC and terminal growth rate combinations
  • Scenario analysis (base / bull / bear) with one-click switching for committee presentations

Impact

◆Models used to support live investment decisions in the 3M+ portfolio
◆Sensitivity framework highlighted key risk factors before position entry
◆Reusable template adopted by multiple sector teams across SIAS
