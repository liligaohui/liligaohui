Automation & AI

Python · Risk · SFU SIAS

Risk & Return Monitoring Automation

A Python automation suite that replaced manual Excel-based risk and performance reporting for the SFU SIAS global equity portfolio — cutting reporting time from days to minutes.

+

The Problem

The SFU SIAS portfolio team was manually compiling weekly risk reports in Excel — pulling data from Bloomberg, calculating active risk, tracking benchmark deviations, and building attribution tables by hand. This process consumed days of analyst time each reporting cycle and introduced inconsistencies from manual data entry.

With a CAD 300M+ portfolio, the delay between data and insights was a real operational risk. We needed a system that could run automatically, produce consistent output, and scale as the portfolio grew.

How I Solved It

I built a Python-based automation pipeline using pandas, numpy, and matplotlib that ingests raw Bloomberg exports and outputs structured risk and performance reports automatically.

  • Automated active risk calculation, tracking, and flagging against IPS thresholds
  • Built return attribution module separating sector vs. stock selection effects
  • Integrated benchmark comparison with automatic deviation alerts
  • Designed output templates that go directly into the weekly investment committee deck

Impact

◆ Reporting time reduced from 2–3 days to under 30 minutes per cycle
◆ Eliminated manual transcription errors across 30+ equity positions
◆ Enabled real-time risk threshold monitoring for the first time
◆ Adopted by the full SIAS portfolio team as the standard reporting workflow

NetSuite · AI · GeoComply

Month-End Close Workflow Automation

Designed and implemented automated workflows in NetSuite for month-end journal entry preparation and account reconciliation — with locally hosted templates and AI-assisted validation.

+

The Problem

Month-end close at GeoComply involved a significant volume of manual journal entries and reconciliations across multiple accounts and entities. The process was error-prone, time-consuming, and difficult to audit consistently — particularly for recurring entries that followed predictable patterns each period.

The team needed a more systematic approach: one that maintained accuracy, reduced manual effort, and produced audit-ready documentation without adding overhead.

How I Solved It

I mapped the existing month-end workflow, identified recurring journal entry types, and built automated templates hosted locally that integrate with NetSuite's journal entry system.

  • Designed structured JE templates for recurring accruals, prepaid amortisation, and intercompany entries
  • Implemented NetSuite saved searches and workflows to pre-populate recurring entries from prior-period data
  • Built a reconciliation framework linking sub-ledger to GL balances with automated variance flagging
  • Integrated AI-assisted review to surface anomalies in account balances before sign-off
  • Hosted templates locally with version control, enabling rollback and period-over-period comparison

Impact

◆ Reduced manual JE preparation time significantly for recurring entry types
◆ Improved reconciliation consistency and produced cleaner audit trails
◆ AI validation layer catches balance anomalies before month-end sign-off
◆ Templates reusable across periods with minimal manual adjustment

Financial Modeling

Portfolio Management · SFU SIAS

CAD 300M+ Equity Portfolio — Risk & Compliance

Managed all risk and compliance functions for the SFU SIAS student-managed equity portfolio, achieving approximately 10% active return with 2.93% annualised risk.

+

The Problem

The SIAS global equity portfolio lacked a systematic risk framework. Compliance with the Investment Policy Statement (IPS) was tracked informally, stress testing was inconsistent, and there was no structured process for identifying when the portfolio drifted outside its mandated risk parameters.

As incoming Risk & Compliance Portfolio Manager, I needed to build this infrastructure from scratch — while simultaneously managing live positions in a real, multi-hundred-million-dollar portfolio.

How I Solved It

I designed and implemented a comprehensive risk management framework covering all dimensions of portfolio risk:

  • Built an active risk monitoring system tracking ex-ante and ex-post tracking error against benchmark
  • Implemented formal stress testing framework covering historical scenarios (2008 GFC, 2020 COVID, 2022 rate shock) and hypothetical shocks
  • Designed scenario analysis tools to model portfolio impact of macro events (recession, rate changes, FX moves)
  • Led monthly IPS compliance reviews, producing structured reports for the investment committee
  • Coordinated with sector managers to ensure position sizing stayed within mandate constraints

Impact

◆ ~10% active return achieved with only 2.93% annualised tracking risk
◆ Zero IPS compliance breaches during tenure as Risk Manager
◆ First formal stress testing framework adopted in SIAS history
◆ Risk reporting automated via Python (see Risk Automation project)
DCF · Valuation · Equity Research

DCF Valuation Models

Built bottom-up DCF models for equity investment decisions within the SIAS portfolio — covering revenue drivers, capital structure assumptions, and multi-scenario sensitivity analysis.

+

The Problem

Investment decisions within the SIAS portfolio required rigorous valuation support. Sector analysts needed DCF models that were not just technically correct, but robust enough to withstand scrutiny in investment committee presentations — and flexible enough to stress-test key assumptions quickly.

Generic DCF templates were insufficient. Each sector and company required a model tailored to its business model, growth drivers, and capital structure.

How I Solved It

I built modular, bottom-up DCF models adaptable across sectors:

  • Revenue build from volume and pricing drivers, not simple growth rate assumptions
  • WACC built from first principles — cost of equity via CAPM, cost of debt from credit spreads
  • Terminal value calculated via both Gordon Growth and EV/EBITDA exit multiple methods for cross-validation
  • Full sensitivity tables across WACC and terminal growth rate combinations
  • Scenario analysis (base / bull / bear) with one-click switching for investment committee presentations

Impact

◆ Models used to support live investment decisions in the 300M+ portfolio
◆ Sensitivity framework highlighted key risk factors before position entry
◆ Reusable template adopted by multiple sector teams across SIAS

Case Competition · Finance · 2025

RBC × Rotman Sustainable Finance Case Competition 2025

Developed a proprietary scoring framework integrating MSCI data and fundamental analysis, with a stewardship engagement plan for underperforming portfolio companies.

+

The Problem

The RBC × Rotman case challenged teams to build a sustainable investing framework for a real institutional portfolio — integrating non-financial factors into fundamental analysis without sacrificing financial rigour.

The core tension: off-the-shelf frameworks treat all sectors the same. We needed an approach that was sector-aware, data-driven, and actionable — not just a checkbox exercise.

How I Solved It

I led development of a sector-adjusted scoring framework built on MSCI data, layered with fundamental analysis:

  • Scored companies on material factors weighted by sector relevance, not uniformly across all categories
  • Integrated scores into valuation framework as a cost-of-capital adjustment — companies with weaker scores faced higher discount rates
  • Identified underperforming portfolio companies and designed a structured stewardship engagement plan with specific targets and timelines
  • Presented recommendations to an RBC judging panel with full quantitative backing

Impact

◆ Presented a fully integrated scoring and valuation framework to RBC judges
◆ Sector-adjusted scoring approach praised for analytical depth
◆ Stewardship plan included concrete engagement milestones and accountability metrics
